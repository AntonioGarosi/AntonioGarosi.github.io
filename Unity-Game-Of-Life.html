An interactive Game Of Life with Unity

I’ve been obsessed since a long, long time with cellular automata, and discovering that developing a version of the famous Conway’s Game Of Life is often considered a benchmark for testing someone’s coding skills was the perfect gimmick to make my own and get more confident with the Unity IDE.

Unity offers an amazing and versatile toolset whose potential isn’t limited to the development of videogames and especially the UI tools can give you a high degree of freedom regarding what you can do. That’s how I managed to craft a web app that is asset-less (no external plugins and no media except for a built-in sliced button sprite and the Arial font), highly operative and relatively resource efficient.

Here it is:

Conway's Game Of Life

You can interact in real time or in pause with mouse and keyboard in many ways, like resizing the lattice, changing the state of the cells, varying the evolution speed or skipping a desired amount generations. You can check the commands by pressing the Escape button.

Depending on browser and computer specs there might be some slow down or halt in the loading of the app. Please, if you see no sign of update within 10-20 seconds consider refrehsing the page.
